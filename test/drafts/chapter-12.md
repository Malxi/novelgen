# 真相的代价

林渊 在 秘密会议 中 发现了 真相

The sub-basiment was a tomb of steel and silence, save for the rhythmic thrum of the ventilation shafts that vibrated against the floorboards. Lin Yuan stood before the holographic table, his breath fogging the cold glass. He felt it then—the heat in his palms. Not the kind that came from muscle fatigue, but a deep, resonant thrumming that vibrated from his marrow outward, a phantom engine running in his own blood.

"The resonance is syncing, isn't it?" Zhang Jie's voice cut through the sterile air, low and dangerous. Zhang Jie stood in the shadows, his silhouette sharp against the flickering blue projections of the core data. The tension between them was a physical weight, heavy and electric, marking the forging of their alliance.

Lin Yuan didn't look away. Inside him, the process was accelerating. The Driver Gene was screaming, pushing its boundaries. He knew the statistics: Level One to Two, Resonance Class. The physical upgrade was undeniable. But the sensation wasn't just mechanical; it was biological. A second heartbeat synchronized with the hum of the room. The veins in his arms glowed faintly violet, tracking the invisible current of the Void Storm.

"It's not just an upgrade, Master," Zhang Jie said, stepping out of the shadows.
"It's a trap," Lin Yuan countered, his voice rough against the cold metal of the room. The Mech Core felt heavier now, demanding more than just fuel. The Elite Class designation wasn't just a label; it required the sacrifice of the user to sustain the machine.

The hologram shifted. Red lines crisscrossed the map, revealing the source of the Void Storm.

"The truth isn't that the storm protects us," Zhang Jie said, his eyes locking with Lin Yuan's. "It's that it feeds on us. This is the cost."

Lin Yuan's grip on the table tightened until his knuckles turned white. The data projected the origin point: not a weapon, but a life-sustaining battery. The Void Storm Rules weren't physics—they were laws imposed on blood.

"You're telling me we're not fighting for Earth?" Lin Yuan asked, his voice dropping to a whisper, heavy with the weight of the revelation.

"We're fighting to become the fuel," Zhang Jie replied, a cold smile curling his lips. The relationship shifted here; Lin Yuan was no longer just a subordinate. He was the variable the system needed to control.

The revelation hit Lin Yuan like a physical blow. The Gene within him wasn't just an evolution; it was the first drop of blood spilt into the system. He felt the connection snap into place. He wasn't just a pilot; he was the key. The Core began to hum in response to his own pulse. The hologram flickered, the truth settling into the room's atmosphere. The cost was high, and it would never be free.

Lin Yuan looked down at his hand, where a faint, violet light pulsed, matching the rhythm of the Void Storm.

"So," Lin Yuan whispered, the weight of the decision pressing on his chest, "the Iron Wings are not ships."

Zhang Jie nodded, a shadow passing over his face. "They are the cage."

The air around them began to crackle, a prelude to the storm. Lin Yuan closed his eyes, feeling the surge. The rules had changed, but the war was about to begin.
