# 训练与磨合

林渊 在 训练场 完成了 基础训练

The wind screamed outside the reinforced viewport, a harsh reminder of the void waiting beyond the training dome. Inside the cockpit, the air was thick with static. Lin Yuan gripped the control deck, his knuckles white.

"Core sync failed," he muttered. His voice was tight, echoing in the small, cramped space.

The mecha's systems screamed in his HUD, a warning light blinking red. The Resonance Gene inside his skull hummed faintly, but it wasn't enough to bridge the gap between human will and machine logic. Lin Yuan felt the friction of the two worlds trying to merge.

"Focus on the signal," he gritted out, forcing his breathing to calm. He closed his eyes, channeling all his energy into the neural link.

Outside, the storm intensified. Lightning struck the outer hull, illuminating the desolate battlefield with strobe flashes. Inside, the core began to vibrate violently. It wasn't just a sound anymore; it was a command. A pulse of pure energy surged through his spine, bypassing the barriers.

*System Notification:* CORE LEVEL: 0 -> 1 (Tactical).

The interface stabilized. The red light turned a calm, steady blue. *System Notification:* DRIVER GENE: 0 -> 1 (Perception).

Lin Yuan sat back, a breathless exhale leaving his lips. The sensation was overwhelming. He could now hear the mechanical heartbeat of the mecha, feel the flow of coolant, understand the weight of every panel. No longer just a pilot, he felt like a conductor.

"Status?" he said to the AI, his voice steady.

"Your tactical capabilities have increased," the voice replied coldly. "The simulation is resetting. Next mission: Full combat simulation."

Lin Yuan looked at the viewport. The storm had passed, leaving a dark ocean of stars. He touched the side of the glass, the cold biting through his glove. This was just the beginning.

"Start the engine," he ordered.

The mecha groaned, its metal bones shifting. But Lin Yuan didn't flinch. He smiled, a faint, sharp expression in the dark cockpit. He wasn't just training anymore. He was waking up with the machine.

The first test had passed, but the real enemy was still out there.
